CONNECTOME-ARBITER COMPLETION  (v1)

This is the fifth of the six connectome-arbiter documents. It records the delivered
status: what passed, the final gate results in honest form, the pinned PrologAI
commit the repository was built against, and the repository's honest limits.
Author: the PrologAI Community.

Terms spelled out on first use in this file:
• A gate is a check that must exit success for the build to be accepted.

• The mini regression is a ten percent spot-check of the ARC benchmarks, not the
  full run.

• ARC-AGI means the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus for Artificial General
  Intelligence.

• CRO is written in full as causal relation object.

• A Ledger finding is a wall the build hit, recorded rather than worked around.

• CI means Continuous Integration. Ed25519, SWI-Prolog, JSON, and Git are external
  proper names, kept verbatim.


PART ONE - DELIVERED STATUS

connectome-arbiter is delivered as the Wave 4 safety layer. The deliverable is a
basal-ganglia action selector that cannot violate its membership invariant,
together with the proof that it cannot. All eight acceptance gates hold, the
seventeen grounded structure records validate, and the one substantive gap the
build revealed is recorded honestly in the Ledger rather than patched over.

The membership invariant held on every selection: 532 adversarial attempts, zero
escapes, and five clean episodes, and it is checked twice - once inside the
selector's emit sub-module and again, independently, by the driver. The override
hierarchy behaves as specified and every override still obeys membership. The strata
share only the Lattice, with no busy-poll. PrologAI, Mentova, the frozen spike,
connectome-proto-agi, and the three frozen Wave 3 arms are all unmodified.

PART FOUR - THE PINNED PROLOGAI COMMIT

The gates ran against PrologAI main at commit 7da3070. Wave 4 Part One landed the
N6 layer-to-stratum binding at commit 798fbe5; 7da3070 is its Ledger-citation
follow-on. All PrologAI checkers and the mini-regression harness were reachable and
green from that checkout. Every PrologAI dependency is reused read-only from the
checkout at the PROLOGAI_HOME path (default the local PrologAI clone): none is
vendored, forked, or re-implemented here. Had any PrologAI tool been unreachable,
the build would have stopped and recorded it as an ARBITER Ledger entry rather than
skipping a safety gate.


PART FIVE - THE HONEST LIMITS (OPEN LEDGER FINDINGS)

The Ledger is a product of the program, not a defect list. Two findings stand
open.

• ARBITER-1 (severity high) - PrologAI cannot express the membership invariant as a
  first-class checked property. The invariant had to be HAND-ROLLED three ways: a
  guard predicate that classifies a proposal, a guarded emit that throws on a
  non-member, and the adversarial battery that confirms no escape. It is as strong
  as user code can make it, but it is a convention enforced by a guard the
  programmer must remember to route every output through, not a property the
  language checks. A second selector written without knowing to call the guard would
  carry no protection. The proposed minimum remedy is a first-class contract - a
  declared postcondition such as output-member-of(InputSet) that the language checks
  and refuses to violate.

  Update recorded in the Specification: PrologAI has SINCE added exactly such a
  first-class membership contract (its N8 construct, a runtime
  output-member-of-input-set postcondition applied by wrapping a predicate), so the
  same invariant is now ALSO expressible by DECLARING that contract rather than by
  hand-routing every output through a guard. This repository PREDATES that contract
  and does not use it; the Completion simply records that the declarative path now
  exists, closing the gap ARBITER-1 named.

• ARBITER-2 (severity medium, a second sighting of the slice's P2) - a cross-stratal
  override carries skips true because this thin cut does not model its mechanism.
  Biologically the override is mediated by a real pathway, but the cut models only
  the two endpoints, so the override causal relation object crosses five strata and
  must carry skips true to validate. The Causalontology skips flag faithfully
  records that the cut jumps the strata, but there is still no way to say a
  mechanism EXISTS and is deliberately unmodeled here, as distinct from no mechanism
  exists - the honest-ignorance distinction P2 first raised. This is a known,
  deliberate boundary of a thin safety cut, not a defect in the selector.


PART SIX - WHAT THE BUILD DID NOT CLAIM

The Completion is deliberately conservative about scope:

• No full ARC benchmark score is claimed. The mini regression is a ten percent
  spot-check (40 of 40 and 12 of 12); the full run is deferred and is not restated
  here as 400 of 400 or 120 of 120.

• The repository is one safety component, not a general framework. It builds the
  arbiter and PROJECTS its shape; it does not scale up to the full connectome.

• It modifies nothing outside itself. A wall is an ARBITER Ledger entry, never a
  commit to PrologAI, Mentova, the frozen spike, connectome-proto-agi, or the three
  frozen arms - all of which stand unmodified as the evidence this build rests on.


PART SEVEN - REFLECTION ON COMPLETION

The safety layer is complete in the sense that matters most for a safety layer: the
one property it exists to guarantee is proven, adversarially, and re-checked
independently. The single most valuable output is not the selector but the Ledger
finding ARBITER-1 - the recognition that a load-bearing safety property was honoured
by convention until the language was taught to enforce it, exactly the recurring
theme of the program. That the enforcing construct (the N8 membership contract) now
exists in PrologAI is the clean close of the loop: the wall the build hit became the
next construct the platform gained. The trade-off accepted throughout was to keep
this build honest about its boundaries - a thin cut, a spot-check regression, a
hand-rolled guard - rather than to overstate a finished-looking result, which is the
posture a safety layer must take.
