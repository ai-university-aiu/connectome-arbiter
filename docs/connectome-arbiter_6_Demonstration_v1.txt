CONNECTOME-ARBITER DEMONSTRATION  (v1)

This is the sixth of the six connectome-arbiter documents, and the owner's addition
beyond the five SPARC phases. It is a PLAN of intent for demonstrating the
repository - what to run, what each step shows, what capabilities it exhibits, and
what a viewer should conclude. It is a plan, not a finished demonstration. Author:
the PrologAI Community.

Terms spelled out on first use in this file:
• SPARC means Specification, Pseudocode, Architecture, Refinement, Completion.

• A gate is a check that must exit success for the build to be accepted.

• Glass-box means the system narrates its own reasoning as it runs, so a viewer can
  watch each step rather than trust a black box.

• The mini regression is a ten percent spot-check of the ARC benchmarks.

• ARC-AGI means the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus for Artificial General
  Intelligence. CRO is written in full as causal relation object. Ed25519,
  SWI-Prolog, JSON, and Git are external proper names, kept verbatim.


PART ONE - WHAT A DEMONSTRATION SHOULD PROVE

The demonstration has one job: convince a viewer that the selector CANNOT emit an
action nobody offered, and that this is proven rather than asserted. Everything else
- the grounded anatomy, the layered structure, the Lattice coordination - is
supporting evidence for that single claim. A viewer who leaves believing only that
"it usually picks sensibly" has missed the point; the point is that the one
dangerous failure mode is impossible to express, and there is a battery that tries
to express it and fails every time.


PART TWO - PREPARATION

Before the demonstration:

• Ensure a PrologAI checkout is present and set PROLOGAI_HOME to it (default the
  local PrologAI clone). Every gate that uses a PrologAI-owned tool reads it from
  there, read-only.

• Confirm SWI-Prolog 9.x is installed.

• Work from the repository root so the bin/ wrappers resolve their paths.

• Have the README, the LEDGER, and these six documents open, because the
  demonstration is as much about the honest Ledger as about the green gates.


PART THREE - THE DEMONSTRATION SEQUENCE

Run the gates in an order that tells a story: first prove the invariant cannot be
broken, then show the selector working, then confirm the structure around it.

Step one - the flagship gate. Run bin/check_membership.sh. This is the moment the
demonstration turns on. The battery makes 532 attempts to force an invented emit -
across every candidate subset, the pipeline, the override, and a TAMPERED gained set
that carries a top-priority action nobody offered - and reports the tallies:
attempts inspected, inventions refused, and escapes. What to point at: the escape
count is zero, and hundreds of inventions were refused outright. What it shows: the
selector is structurally incapable of returning an unoffered action; the guard
throws instead.

Step two - the live selector. Run bin/run_arbiter.sh. The three strata spawn as
background threads and hand off only by numbered phase cue over the Lattice. The
glass-box trace narrates five episodes: a normal selection (highest priority wins),
a vetoed selection (fall back to the next OFFERED action), a total veto (the safe
outcome is no-selection), a single candidate, and a high-dopamine case (the gain
raises vigour but never changes WHICH action wins). What to point at: after each
episode the driver prints its INDEPENDENT invariant check, and the final verdict is
that the invariant held on five of five with zero actor-to-actor references and no
busy-poll. What it shows: the guarantee does not rest on the in-selector guard
alone; the driver re-checks it.

Step three - the closure discipline. Run bin/check_no_coupling.sh. What to point at:
zero cross-tick references - no stratum's runtime tick names another's. What it
shows: the strata are genuinely decoupled; they share only the Lattice.

Step four - the layered structure. Run bin/check_layers.sh and then
bin/check_layer_binding.sh. What to point at: zero upward edges, and every stratum
pack's layer order-consistent with its stratum's ordinal (synaptic 7 to layer 1,
region 9 to layer 2, community 14 to layer 3). What it shows: the override hierarchy
fits PrologAI's strict layer rule and the enforced N6 binding without strain - and
this is the first real build to rest on that binding.

Step five - the grounded anatomy. Run bin/validate_structure.sh. What to point at:
all seventeen records valid against Causalontology 2.0.0, the override causal
relation object classified as a clean skip, and its Ed25519 signature verified. What
it shows: the selector's own anatomy is content-addressed, schema-valid, and signed
- not prose but data.

Step six - the in-pack tests. Run bin/run_tests.sh. What to point at: every pack's
PLUnit suite passes, and the selector and override suites are written to TRY to break
the invariant. What it shows: the isolation the Wave 3 verdict promised is real -
each of the selector's five sub-modules is exercised on its own.

Step seven - the regression spot-check. Note the mini regression result: ARC-AGI-1
40 of 40 and ARC-AGI-2 12 of 12, a TEN PERCENT spot-check, with the full regression
deferred. What to point at, out loud: this is 40 of 40 and 12 of 12, NOT 400 of 400
or 120 of 120. What it shows: the surrounding platform did not regress, stated
honestly at the resolution actually run.


PART FOUR - THE CAPABILITIES ON DISPLAY

A viewer who watches the full sequence sees five capabilities:

• A safety invariant enforced by construction - an emit that can only return a
  member, a no-selection, or a throw.

• Adversarial self-proof - a battery that attacks the invariant from four angles and
  reports zero escapes.

• Defence in depth - the invariant checked inside the selector, filtered again in the
  override, and re-checked independently in the driver.

• Decoupled, glass-box coordination - three strata that hand off only by numbered
  cue over a shared Lattice, narrating every hop.

• Grounded, signed anatomy - seventeen Causalontology 2.0.0 records, one clean
  cross-stratal skip, one Ed25519 signature, resting on the enforced layer-to-stratum
  binding.


PART FIVE - THE HONEST-LIMITS PORTION OF THE DEMONSTRATION

A demonstration that showed only green gates would be dishonest about this
repository. Walk the viewer through the LEDGER as the second half:

• ARBITER-1 - the invariant had to be HAND-ROLLED, because at build time PrologAI
  had no first-class way to declare "the output of this predicate must be a member of
  this input set". Show that the guarantee, though proven, is a convention a
  programmer must remember to route every output through. Then show the close: the
  Specification records that PrologAI has SINCE added exactly that first-class
  membership contract (its N8 construct), so the invariant is now ALSO expressible by
  declaration - this repository predates it and does not use it.

• ARBITER-2 - the contextual override crosses five strata with no modeled mechanism,
  so it honestly carries skips true. Show that the skip flag records "this cut jumps
  the strata" but still cannot say "a mechanism exists and is deliberately unmodeled
  here" as distinct from "no mechanism exists".

The intended effect: the viewer should trust the result MORE for having seen the
gaps named, not less.


PART SIX - WHAT A VIEWER SHOULD CONCLUDE

By the end, a viewer should be able to state four things without hedging:

• The selector cannot emit an action nobody offered, and there is a 532-attempt
  adversarial proof, plus an independent per-episode re-check, that it cannot.

• The winning Wave 3 decomposition (one pack per stratum outside, one construct per
  sub-module inside) recovered per-construct testability and fit the layer rule and
  the enforced N6 binding without strain.

• The selector's anatomy is grounded, signed data, not prose - and PrologAI is reused
  read-only, unmodified, from a pinned checkout.

• The build is honest about its boundaries - a thin cut, a spot-check regression, a
  hand-rolled guard whose enforcing construct the platform has since gained - which
  is the posture a safety layer must take.


PART SEVEN - REFLECTION ON THE DEMONSTRATION PLAN

The demonstration is ordered to make one point land first and hardest: the flagship
membership battery runs before anything else, so a viewer sees the impossibility
proof before seeing the selector do anything pleasant. The trade-off is that the
narrative leads with a negative result (nothing escaped) rather than a positive one
(a sensible selection), which is less satisfying to watch but truer to what a safety
layer is for. The honest-limits portion is placed at the end, not hidden, so the
green gates and the open Ledger are read as one story rather than two.
