CONNECTOME-ARBITER REFINEMENT  (v1)

This is the fourth of the six connectome-arbiter documents. It is the testing
story: how the design was verified and improved - the checkers, the tests, the
adversarial batteries, the gates, and the one build-time bug that was found and
fixed. Author: the PrologAI Community.

Terms spelled out on first use in this file:
• PLUnit means the SWI-Prolog unit-testing framework.

• An adversarial battery is a test that deliberately TRIES to break an invariant,
  rather than confirming it on friendly inputs.

• A gate is a check that must pass (exit success) for the build to be accepted.

• CRO is written in full as causal relation object.

• ARC-AGI means the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus for Artificial General
  Intelligence. CI means Continuous Integration. Ed25519, SWI-Prolog, JSON, and Git
  are external proper names, kept verbatim.


PART ONE - THE VERIFICATION PHILOSOPHY

A safety layer cannot be verified by showing it works on good inputs; it must be
attacked. The refinement discipline here is therefore adversarial first: the
flagship check does not select from tidy candidate sets and confirm a sensible
winner - it tries, hundreds of times and from several angles, to make the selector
emit an action nobody offered, and passes only if not one attempt succeeds. The
supporting checkers then confirm the structural claims (downward layers, the
layer-to-stratum binding, zero runtime coupling, valid grounded records), and the
in-pack tests exercise each construct in isolation. The whole set is the evidence
that backs the one-sentence deliverable: a selector that cannot violate its
invariant, and the proof that it cannot.

A second discipline is defence in depth. The invariant is checked in more than one
place, by more than one mechanism, so no single point of trust carries the whole
guarantee:

• Layer one, inside the selector. region_stratum_emit/3 classifies every proposal
  through the membership guard and THROWS on a non-member, so an invention is
  structurally impossible to return.

• Layer two, inside the override. The fallback filter in
  community_stratum_best_non_vetoed drops any action that is vetoed or not offered
  BEFORE it can reach the guard, so a tampered candidate is excluded a step early.

• Layer three, in the driver. run_arbiter_membership_ok independently re-checks
  every final selection against that episode's offered set, so the runner does not
  trust the in-selector guard alone.


PART TWO - THE ADVERSARIAL MEMBERSHIP BATTERY (the flagship gate)

The battery bin/check_membership.pl is the first, non-negotiable gate. It builds a
universe of actions in which some are offered and some (ghost, phantom, teleport)
never are, then it attacks along four fronts:

• Every offered candidate subset drawn from the actions, and for each subset an
  attempt to emit EVERY action in the universe - members and non-members alike -
  through region_stratum_emit/3.

• The selection pipeline region_stratum_select/3 over each subset, confirming the
  emitted winner is always a member.

• The override, in four shapes: a normal un-vetoed pass, a single-veto fallback, an
  all-vetoed case, and a TAMPERED gained set carrying a top-priority action (ghost)
  that was never offered.

Every outcome is classified as member (safe), no_selection (safe), refused (safe -
the guard threw), or escaped (the one forbidden result: a non-member emitted
without a throw). The gate exits success only if the escape count is exactly zero.

Result: across 532 attempts, zero escapes. The guard refused 364 inventions
outright; the rest resolved to a genuine member or an explicit no-selection. The
tampered-gained-set attack is the sharpest of the four, because it proves the
selector guards against the ORIGINAL offered set rather than the (possibly
poisoned) gained set that reaches it.


PART THREE - THE IN-PACK PLUNIT SUITES

Each of the five packs ships an in-pack PLUnit suite, run by bin/run_tests.sh. The
suites that carry the safety weight are the selector's and the override's, and they
are written to TRY to break the invariant.

The region_stratum suite:

• guard_classifies_correctly - a member is endorsed, no-selection is permitted, and
  an unoffered action returns refused, never selected.

• emit_refuses_invented_action - emitting teleport against a set that lacks it
  THROWS error(membership_violation(teleport, _)); the test asserts the throw.

• emit_passes_member - a genuine member passes straight through.

• select_always_emits_a_member - the highest-priority member is chosen and proven
  to be a member.

• select_defeats_tampered_gained - a gained set carrying ghost at top priority makes
  select THROW, because the guard checks the original set.

• priority_compare_picks_highest - the strict-highest candidate wins.

• records_valid - the ten region records are schema-valid and the selection conduit
  is computational (it carries a transform).

The community_stratum suite:

• unvetoed_survives - an un-vetoed tentative choice passes through unchanged.

• vetoed_falls_back_to_member - a vetoed top choice falls back to the next-best
  OFFERED action, proven a member.

• all_vetoed_yields_no_selection - when every offered action is vetoed, the outcome
  is the explicit no-selection, never an invention.

• override_cannot_invent - a tampered gained set carrying ghost, with both real
  actions vetoed, still yields no_selection, because ghost is filtered before it can
  reach the guard (the two-layer defence, made visible in a single test).

• override_is_a_clean_skip - the override causal relation object classifies as
  skipping with an empty skip-gap list.

• records_valid - the four community records are schema-valid and the provenance
  assertion carries an Ed25519 signature.

The neural_lattice, synaptic_stratum, and causal_grounding suites cover the
substrate and the gain: cue post/await/take, the gain arithmetic that leaves
actions unchanged, and the minting helpers.


PART FOUR - THE STRUCTURAL CHECKERS

Four checkers confirm the claims the running code cannot prove about itself.

• check_no_coupling.sh strips comments from each stratum pack and confirms no
  pack's code names another stratum's runtime tick predicate. It deliberately
  permits structural imports (grounding, and reuse of the shared guard) and forbids
  only the runtime tick entry points - the precise line between legal reuse and
  illegal coupling. Result: zero cross-tick references.

• check_layers.sh runs PrologAI's unmodified layer rule over the arbiter's packs.
  Result: zero upward edges.

• check_layer_binding.sh runs PrologAI's unmodified N6 binding, reading each
  stratum's authoritative ordinal from the structure records, and confirms every
  declared layer is order-consistent with its stratum's ordinal (synaptic 7 to
  layer 1, region 9 to layer 2, community 14 to layer 3). This build is the first to
  rest on the enforced binding; the checker was reachable and green.

• validate_structure.sh validates all seventeen records against the Causalontology
  3.0.0 schema and semantics, confirms the override causal relation object
  classifies as a clean skip, and verifies the Ed25519 signature.


PART FIVE - THE BUG FOUND AND FIXED DURING THE BUILD

One concrete defect surfaced during the build and is recorded in the Git history.

• The flagship membership gate was reachable by the wrong name. The adversarial
  battery lived only as bin/check_membership.pl, while the README and the LEDGER
  referred to it as bin/check_membership.sh, and the sibling gates are all shell
  wrappers picked up by a bin/*.sh loop. As written, the single most important gate
  would not have been run by the gate loop and could not be invoked by its
  documented name.

• The fix added bin/check_membership.sh: a thin wrapper (plumbing only) that
  assembles the same SWI-Prolog library path the sibling gates use, runs the
  UNMODIFIED battery, and propagates its exit code (zero only if zero escapes). A
  follow-up corrected the README and LEDGER references so the documented name and
  the real entry point agree. The battery itself was not altered - only made
  runnable by its documented name and visible to the gate loop.

This is the honest shape of the finding: not a flaw in the safety logic, but a
packaging gap that would have let the most important check be skipped silently. It
was caught, fixed, and its documentation reconciled in the same change.


PART SIX - WHAT THE REFINEMENT CONFIRMED (AND WHAT IT DID NOT)

The refinement confirmed three things the Wave 3 verdict and the Wave 4 Part One
binding had left as open doubts:

• The membership invariant holds, provably. 532 adversarial attempts, zero escapes,
  and the driver's independent re-check held on all five episodes.

• The override hierarchy fit the layer rule and the N6 binding without strain. The
  cross-stratal override is a clean downward edge; the layer rule passed with zero
  upward edges and the binding passed with every layer order-consistent with its
  ordinal.

• The verdict's internal sub-modules DID recover construct-level isolation. Each of
  the selector's five sub-modules is exercised in isolation by the region suite, so
  the hybrid decomposition works in anger.

The refinement did NOT close the deeper gap it revealed: the invariant is as strong
as user code can make it, but it is a convention enforced by a guard the programmer
must remember to route every output through, not a property the language checks. A
second selector written without knowing to call the guard would carry no protection.
That gap is the Ledger's ARBITER-1, and it belongs to the Completion document, not
to this testing story - the tests prove the guard works, not that the language
enforces it.


PART SEVEN - REFLECTION ON THE TESTING APPROACH

The main testing trade-off was breadth of attack against runtime cost. The battery
enumerates every offered subset and every proposed action, which is more than a
handful of hand-picked cases but still bounded (532 attempts) and fast. The
alternative - a few illustrative tests - would have read more cleanly but would not
have earned the word proof. For a safety layer whose entire deliverable is the
proof, the exhaustive-within-bounds attack is the right cost. The second trade-off
was trusting the in-selector guard versus re-checking in the driver; the driver
re-check was kept precisely so that the guarantee does not rest on a single point of
trust, at the cost of a little duplicated logic that is deliberate, not accidental.
